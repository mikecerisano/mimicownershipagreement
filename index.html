MIMIC EQUITY AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made and entered into as of the date of execution by and between Michael Cerisano, Arielle Murphy, Kevin Burns, and Matthew Woods (collectively, the "Partners") regarding their shared ownership and decision-making structure for the short film MIMIC and any future adaptations, licensing, or sales derived from it.

1. Ownership & Equity Distribution

1.1 The ownership of MIMIC is structured as follows:

  • Investors collectively own 26.25% of MIMIC (20% to Dr. Srihari Naidu and 6.25% to Bailey Richardson).
  • The remaining 73.75% is equally distributed among the four Partners.
  • Each Partner (Michael Cerisano, Arielle Murphy, Kevin Burns, and Matthew Woods) owns an equal 18.4375% stake in MIMIC.

1.2 This ownership applies to any revenue, licensing, sales, or adaptations related to MIMIC but does not extend to other projects produced under the Red Fox Features banner.

1.3 The investors' ownership is limited to revenue generated directly from MIMIC. They do not hold any rights over decisions regarding licensing, adaptation, or sale of the intellectual property beyond standard profit-sharing agreements.

2. Revenue & Profit Distribution

2.1 Any revenue generated from MIMIC after production and post-production costs are fully recouped shall be distributed as follows:

  • 26.25% allocated to investors, divided in proportion to their contributions (20% to Dr. Srihari Naidu and 6.25% to Bailey Richardson).
  • 73.75% allocated equally among the four Partners (Michael Cerisano, Arielle Murphy, Kevin Burns, and Matthew Woods).

2.2 If MIMIC is sold, optioned, or licensed, any proceeds from such deals shall be distributed according to the ownership percentages stated in section 1.1.

2.3 Revenue from any future adaptations (e.g., feature film, TV series, anthology licensing) shall be divided equally among the four Partners.

3. Decision-Making & Voting Rights

3.1 All creative and business decisions regarding MIMIC, including licensing, distribution, and adaptation into other media, require a simple majority vote (3 out of 4 Partners).

3.2 Any decisions that do not involve investor ownership (e.g., selling MIMIC, adapting it into a feature, licensing the concept) shall be made exclusively by the four Partners.

3.3 Arielle Murphy, as owner of Red Fox Features LLC, retains administrative rights to handle business filings, tax reporting, and contractual obligations related to MIMIC but does not have unilateral decision-making power regarding distribution or creative control.

3.4 If one Partner decides to leave or relinquish their stake, their ownership must first be offered to the remaining Partners before any external sale.

4. Legal & Miscellaneous Provisions

4.1 No Guaranteed Returns: All Partners acknowledge that independent film investment is speculative and returns are not guaranteed.

4.2 Dispute Resolution: Any disputes arising under this Agreement shall first be addressed through internal mediation. If mediation fails, arbitration under Delaware law shall be the final resolution.

4.3 Modification Clause: Any changes to this Agreement must be made in writing and signed by all four Partners.

4.4 Governing Law: This Agreement is governed by the laws of Delaware, where Red Fox Features LLC is registered.
